# EverFi — Financial History Findings

**Sources:** Blackbaud acquisition PR [fn:7], Blackbaud sale PR [fn:8], Technical.ly [fn:9], EverFi $40M raise [fn:10], Crunchbase [fn:16], Vector Solutions [fn:17], Yahoo Finance [fn:19]

## Founding & Early Funding

- Founded 2008 by Tom Davidson and Jon Chapman in Washington, DC
- Origin story: started in a Georgetown apartment (1225 Potomac St NW)
- First venture round (~2010): $11 million
- Second round (~2012): ~$10 million from Jeff Bezos (Bezos Expeditions) and Evan Williams (Twitter founder) and Eric Schmidt (Google)
- $40 million round: Led by Bezos Expeditions; co-investors NEA, Tomorrow Ventures (Schmidt), Rethink Education
- Total raised: $251 million+

## Pre-Acquisition Divestiture

- **July 2021:** EverFi's Higher Education business unit sold to **Vector Solutions** (Florida-based)
- **Price:** $100 million
- **Assets sold:** Online compliance training for colleges and universities including AlcoholEdu, Haven (campus sexual assault prevention), Campus Prevention Network
- **Note:** This significantly reduced EverFi's footprint before the Blackbaud deal

## Blackbaud Acquisition

- **Closed:** January 3, 2022
- **Acquirer:** Blackbaud, Inc. (BLKB, NASDAQ) — cloud software company for social good organizations, headquartered Charleston, SC
- **Total value:** ~$750 million
- **Structure:**
  - ~$450 million cash
  - 3,844,423 shares of Blackbaud common stock (~$300 million value)
- **EverFi projected revenue (2022):** ~$120 million
- **Projected growth rate:** ~20% year-over-year
- **Strategic rationale:** Doubled Blackbaud's total addressable market to $20B+; "Impact-as-a-Service" combined with Blackbaud's YourCause CSR platform
- **Blackbaud CEO Mike Gianoni quote:** Combined entity would be "a leading partner to help corporations drive meaningful social impact"

## Blackbaud Ownership: Deterioration

- **Revenue performance:** EVERFI segment saw a **26% drop in revenue** under Blackbaud ownership
- **Impairment charge:** Blackbaud recorded a **~$415 million noncash impairment** on the EVERFI asset group due to underperformance
- **Context:** The impairment alone nearly equaled the entire cash purchase price

## Sale / Divestiture

- **Announced:** January 2, 2025
- **Closed:** December 31, 2024
- **Buyer:** Unnamed private investment firm (not affiliated with Blackbaud)
- **Price:** "Nominal amount" — no specific figure disclosed
- **Blackbaud retained:** YourCause and Impact Edge solutions
- **Dale Strange (Blackbaud president of corporate impact) statement:** "Working closely with the buyer and the EVERFI leadership team to provide a seamless transition"
