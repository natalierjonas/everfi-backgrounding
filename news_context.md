# EverFi — Business Model, Government Connections & Risk Context

**Sources:** American Prospect [fn:13], SouthEast Bank [fn:14], OSU effectiveness doubts [fn:15], EverFi White House [fn:11], Enigma Negative News [fn:6], Capital One [fn:18]

## Business Model: "Impact-as-a-Service"

EverFi pioneered what it called "Impact-as-a-Service" (IaaS): corporations — primarily banks, credit unions, and insurance companies — pay EverFi to deploy "free" digital curriculum into K-12 schools and universities. The corporate sponsor gets:
- Brand placement in student-facing educational materials
- Community reinvestment credit (for banks under CRA)
- CSR/ESG reporting metrics ("board-level reporting showing positive societal impact")
- Access to students, teachers, and parents as a marketing audience

Schools receive ostensibly free curriculum.

### The Structural Conflict

As reported by The American Prospect: "Many financial literacy programs are offered to schools for free or at a low cost — often sponsored by banks that say they believe in the mission, but are also intrigued by the marketing and public relations opportunities associated with branded content, with educators, students, and even their parents all representing potential customers."

**Example partnerships in practice:**
- **Capital One:** Sponsors "Modern Money" course — branded Capital One financial literacy content delivered in schools
- **SouthEast Bank:** Paid for EverFi curriculum in 30 Tennessee high schools (2024–25 school year); reached 2,600+ students
- **Scale:** EverFi reached 7,000+ school districts; 21,000+ schools nationwide
- **Sponsoring institutions:** Banks, credit unions, insurance companies, foundations

### The Compliance Training Vertical

Before its sale to Vector Solutions:
- **AlcoholEdu:** Online alcohol education for incoming college freshmen; >500 campuses; largest online course in higher education
- **Haven:** Campus sexual assault prevention; >400 institutions
- Over 6 million combined completions
- Revenue model: universities pay per-student fees for mandatory compliance training

**White House partnership (2014–2016):** EverFi partnered with Obama White House "It's On Us" campus sexual assault initiative, providing Haven at scale across campuses scrambling to comply with Title IX enforcement.

### Criticism: Effectiveness

Oklahoma State researchers and outside experts questioned whether Haven's online format delivered real behavior change, or served primarily as a **legal compliance checkbox** for institutions. The course's completion rate, not behavioral outcomes, typically drives institutional purchasing decisions.
